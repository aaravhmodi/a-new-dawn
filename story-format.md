# STAR WARS: A NEW DAWN Story Format

## Core Direction

The game should feel like:

- Star Wars scale and atmosphere
- a James Bond espionage thriller
- a clear, emotionally readable story even for players who do not know Star Wars lore

That means:

- less jargon
- more context inside the narration
- strong mission structure
- glamorous, dangerous set pieces
- memorable villains and allies
- one major action sequence per episode
- a clean cliffhanger or reveal at the end

## Player Fantasy

The player is **Rylos Cesti**.

Rylos starts as a covert field operative working inside the Empire's intelligence world. He is competent, adaptable, and dangerous, but not yet fully aware of who he is becoming.

The story hook is:

- he is sent into a shadow war
- he uncovers a secret network trying to destabilize both Sith and Jedi
- he discovers he is Force-sensitive
- he must decide whether to remain a tool, become a free agent, or reshape the conflict himself

## Tone Rules

### What to emphasize

- infiltration
- seduction, deception, and coded identities
- luxury hiding corruption
- political danger
- stylish fieldcraft
- morally loaded choices
- escalating revelations

### What to avoid

- dense lore dumps
- unexplained proper nouns every paragraph
- assuming the player already knows every faction or title
- constant lightsaber/Jedi talk from the beginning
- random fan-service cameos with no story purpose

## Vocabulary Rule

Every Star Wars-specific concept should either:

- be understandable from context
- be briefly explained in-world

Example:

Bad:
"The Sith are tracking a holocron through Kaas."

Better:
"A Sith lord, one of the Empire's feared dark-side rulers, is hunting an ancient holocron, a device that stores secrets and teachings."

The player should never need outside lore knowledge to understand the stakes.

## Episode Format

The shipped prototype should have **1 fully polished episode**.

Later episodes can follow the same format, but they should not block release.

Each episode should have **10 prompts/scenes**.

Each episode should also follow the classic Star Wars act rhythm:

- Act I: escape
- Act II: infiltration
- Act III: confrontation

This is the standard structure:

1. Cold open
2. Mission briefing
3. Social infiltration
4. First complication
5. Investigation reveal
6. Midpoint choice
7. Action sequence
8. Fallout and character moment
9. Final confrontation or extraction
10. Ending reveal / cliffhanger

## Three-Act Pattern

The core pacing rule is:

- **Act I gets Rylos out**
- **Act II gets Rylos in**
- **Act III forces Rylos to face someone or something directly**

This should make each episode feel closer to a Star Wars film rather than a loose collection of missions.

### Act I: Escape

Prompts 1 to 3

Act I should begin with instability, danger, or flight.

This does not always mean running away physically. It can mean:

- escaping a compromised position
- escaping a bad identity
- escaping surveillance
- escaping political or criminal pressure

The point is:

- the episode starts in motion
- the player is forced into action quickly
- the mission is not comfortable

### Act II: Infiltration

Prompts 4 to 7

Act II is about getting inside something:

- a place
- an organization
- a person's trust
- a hidden truth

This is the espionage center of the episode.

It should contain:

- disguise or cover work
- social maneuvering
- investigation
- one major action scene as the pressure point

### Act III: Confrontation

Prompts 8 to 10

Act III is where hidden things become direct.

This should end in confrontation with:

- a villain
- a handler
- a moral truth
- a conspiracy reveal
- Rylos's own identity

The confrontation does not always need to be a duel or battle.
Sometimes it should be:

- a choice
- a betrayal
- a reveal
- a psychological break

## 10-Prompt Structure by Act

### Act I

#### 1. Cold open

Begin in danger, flight, or immediate pressure.

#### 2. Mission briefing

Explain the goal in clean, simple terms.

#### 3. Entry choice

The player chooses how to approach the episode's first threshold.

### Act II

#### 4. Social infiltration

Rylos enters the scene behind the scene.

#### 5. First complication

The cover or plan is stressed.

#### 6. Investigation reveal

The mission becomes bigger than it first appeared.

#### 7. Action sequence

This is the episode's set-piece and should be the physical peak of Act II.

### Act III

#### 8. Fallout and character beat

Let the action have consequences.

#### 9. Confrontation

Rylos faces the episode's human or moral center.

#### 10. Cliffhanger or transformation

End with a change that matters.

## What each prompt does

### 1. Cold open

Start with tension immediately.

Examples:

- a surveillance handoff in the rain
- a luxury casino meeting gone wrong
- a smuggler convoy under attack

Goal:

- establish mood
- create immediate curiosity

### 2. Mission briefing

Explain the mission clearly in plain language.

Goal:

- who wants what
- why it matters
- what success looks like

This is where Star Wars jargon should be contextualized.

### 3. Social infiltration

Rylos enters a social, political, or criminal setting.

Examples:

- elite gala
- underworld club
- officer lounge
- diplomatic auction

Goal:

- James Bond energy
- style, disguise, conversation, tension

### 4. First complication

The plan starts slipping.

Examples:

- cover identity is challenged
- rival operative recognizes a detail
- security changes unexpectedly

### 5. Investigation reveal

The player learns something deeper than the mission objective.

Examples:

- the target is bait
- a hidden patron is funding both sides
- the enemy is building a network rather than a weapon

### 6. Midpoint choice

This is the strategic decision point.

Examples:

- trust the handler or keep secrets
- protect an ally or preserve the mission
- save civilians or capture the asset

### 7. Action sequence

Each episode must have exactly one major action set piece.

Examples:

- rooftop pursuit in acid rain
- train fight through a weapons convoy
- stealth breakout from an archive vault
- speeder chase through city traffic

This is the cinematic peak of the episode.

### 8. Fallout and character moment

Slow down after action.

Goal:

- let consequences land
- deepen ally/villain relationships
- give Rylos a reflective beat

### 9. Final confrontation or extraction

The player faces:

- a target
- a betrayal
- a capture risk
- a moral dilemma

This should feel like the mission's final threshold.

### 10. Ending reveal / cliffhanger

Every episode ends with one of:

- a twist
- a betrayal
- a new identity revealed
- a Force-related development
- a direct escalation into the next episode

## Story Spine for the 9 Episodes

This is the baseline storyline.

### Episode I: The False Name

Rylos goes undercover to infiltrate a hidden intelligence leak on Dromund Kaas. He learns a secret cell called Directorate Null is moving against both Sith and Jedi. During the final extraction, he unconsciously uses the Force for the first time.

Action sequence:

- archive breach and escape under lockdown

Ending:

- Rylos realizes something ancient has awakened inside him

### Episode II: The Black Ledger

Rylos infiltrates a luxury finance ring laundering money into political sabotage. He discovers Directorate Null is not just a terror network, but a long-term conspiracy using exhaustion with war to turn worlds against both Force traditions.

Action sequence:

- casino pursuit and vault escape

Ending:

- someone inside Intelligence is leaking his movements

### Episode III: Dead Signal

A field station goes dark. Rylos is sent to recover a missing watcher and decode a signal that appears to predict Jedi and Sith operations before they happen.

Action sequence:

- train ambush across a mountain supply line

Ending:

- Rylos learns the conspiracy has access to a hidden codex of identities and blackmail records

### Episode IV: Velvet Knife

Rylos enters an aristocratic summit where politicians, spies, officers, and crime patrons gather behind masks. He must charm, deceive, and survive long enough to identify the next architect of the conspiracy.

Action sequence:

- ballroom assassination attempt and vertical escape

Ending:

- a trusted ally is exposed as compromised

### Episode V: The Quiet Prison

Rylos is captured or cornered near a black-site detention facility where Directorate Null stores people, data, and leverage. He sees how the conspiracy erases lives and rewrites loyalties.

Action sequence:

- prison break through a collapsing holding complex

Ending:

- Rylos chooses whether to rescue a dangerous prisoner who knows the truth about his past

### Episode VI: The Splinter Hand

Rylos follows the conspiracy into a frontier warzone where both Imperial and anti-Imperial cells are being manipulated into mutual destruction. His Force sensitivity becomes harder to hide.

Action sequence:

- battlefield extraction under aerial bombardment

Ending:

- his handler confronts him about what he is becoming

### Episode VII: The Hidden Republic

Rylos finds a world, faction, or enclave secretly backing Directorate Null's anti-Force ideology. Here he learns the conspiracy's philosophy: if Jedi and Sith vanish, the galaxy may finally know peace, order, and political independence from mystic rule.

Action sequence:

- jungle infiltration and temple collapse

Ending:

- Rylos finds proof that the conspiracy has targeted him since before Episode I

### Episode VIII: No One's Weapon

Rylos turns hunter. He dismantles the network's remaining shields, but every faction now wants control over him, the codex, or the truth. He must decide whom to betray first.

Action sequence:

- urban manhunt with multiple factions closing in

Ending:

- the road to the final mastermind opens

### Episode IX: The Last Shadow

Rylos reaches the hidden leadership of Directorate Null. The final decision is not just whether to destroy them, but whether to expose their methods, inherit their invisibility, or reshape the galaxy's balance through secrecy.

Action sequence:

- final infiltration and command-center collapse

Ending:

- one of several final destinies for Rylos Cesti

## Core Character Roles

These roles should repeat across episodes.

### Rylos Cesti

- lead operative
- sharp, adaptable, increasingly conflicted
- starts as spy, becomes something more dangerous

### Watcher Nine

- handler
- disciplined, intelligent, possibly loyal to the Empire first
- relationship can become trust, suspicion, or emotional tension

### Veska Tal

- field watcher / ally
- pragmatic, dangerous, underworld capable
- can become a trusted partner or a rival survivor

### Warden Karn

- relentless state enforcer
- visible pressure from the system
- a recurring hunter who may become enemy, scapegoat, or uneasy ally

### Directorate Null

- secret anti-Force network
- believes Jedi/Sith conflict has poisoned civilization
- sophisticated, ideological, not cartoonishly evil

## James Bond Elements Checklist

Every episode should include at least 4 of these:

- a cover identity
- a stylish location
- a seductive or dangerous conversation
- a luxury or elite environment hiding corruption
- a gadget, code key, or disguised tool
- a double-cross
- a villain speech or ideological reveal
- an elegant but brutal action sequence

## Choice Design Rules

Each major prompt should offer 3 choices:

- controlled/professional
- risky/aggressive
- independent/deceptive

Choices should change:

- trust
- leverage
- alignment
- which later scenes feel personal

Choices should rarely create completely separate plots. They should mostly create:

- different emotional tone
- different costs
- different relationships
- different payoff scenes later

## Narrative Simplicity Rules

To keep the story readable:

- introduce no more than 2 new named characters in one episode unless one is minor
- define any new faction in one sentence
- every episode should have one clear mission goal
- every episode should have one emotional center
- every episode should end with one sentence that changes the future
- every episode should clearly feel like escape, infiltration, then confrontation

## Recommended Writing Pattern per Scene

For implementation, each prompt should contain:

- scene title
- 1 paragraph of cinematic setup
- 1 paragraph of emotional or situational context
- 3 choices

Action scenes may use:

- 2 short paragraphs instead of one long block

This keeps CLI reading comfortable.

## Implementation Recommendation

Build Episode I, II, and III by hand first using this format.

Do not rely on AI to invent the whole plot structure.

Use AI for:

- phrasing
- scene flavor
- dialogue texture
- recap text

Use your own structure for:

- episode arcs
- reveals
- character roles
- final twists
- action set pieces

That will make the game feel authored instead of random.
